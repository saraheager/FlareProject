Tamagoyaki pan (玉子焼き器)

Also known as makiyakinabe, a tamagoyaki pan is a rectangular pan used to make Japanese rolled omelettes (tamagoyaki).

The rectangular shape of the pan facilitates a constant diameter over the length of the omelette, giving the omelette its bar-like shape once rolled. Most professional pans are made of heavy copper coated or lined with tin, with these materials being preferred for their heat conduction. A cheaper, nonstick variety is a common alternative to the copper pan.
