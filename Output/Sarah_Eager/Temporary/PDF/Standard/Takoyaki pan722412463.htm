Takoyaki pan (たこ焼き器)

A takoyaki pan is typically a griddle made of cast iron with hemispherical molds.

The heavy iron evenly heats the takoyaki, which are turned with a pick during the heating process to pull the uncooked batter to the base of the rounded cavity. Commercial gas-fueled takoyaki cookers are used at Japanese festivals or by street vendors. For home use, electric versions resemble a hotplate; stovetop versions are also available.
