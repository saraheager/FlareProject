Castella cake (カステラ)

Castella is a basic sponge containing only five ingredients. Hailing from Portugal, it was introduced to Nagasaki by Portuguese missionaries.

Prep: 30 min

Cook: 30 min

Serves: 1 large cake

Ingredients

  • 6 eggs

  • 200 g sugar

  • 200 g bread flour, sifted

  • 120 ml honey

  • 2 Tbsp warm water

 

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 160C.
  2. Sift the flour.
  3. Brush the bottom and sides of a loaf pan with oil or melted butter. Line the sides with baking paper.
  4. Add the eggs to the bowl of a stand mixer and whisk until combined.
  5. Pour in the sugar and beat until the eggs increase in volume, approximately 4 times the original volume.
  6. Combine 110 ml honey and 1.5 Tbsp water and add to the egg mixture. Whisk gently until combined.
  7. Add 1/4 of the bread flour and whisk on low speed until just combined. Repeat with the remaining flour, adding 1/4 at a time.
  8. Pour the batter into the pan and use a skewer to remove air bubbles.
  9. Bake in the centre of the oven for 30-35 minutes, or until golden brown.
  10. Once done, turn off the oven and open the door slightly. Leave the cake to cool in the oven for 10 minutes. This will prevent it from collapsing.
  11. Remove from the oven and brush the top with the remaining honey and water.
  12. Turn upside down on cling film. This will give a flat top. Wrap with cling film and transfer to the fridge overnight, keeping the top down.
  13. To serve, bring to room temperature and cut off the ends and sides.
